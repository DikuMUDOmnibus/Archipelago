Archipelago is a based on the CircleMUD 2.2 code (see below). The major modifications by Alastair Neil (Ponder) at The University of Essex Dept. of Psychology, Soraya Ghiasi (Kalliste) at The University of Colorado, Boulder, and Shawn L. Baird (Scarrow). Angus Hamilton (Arg) has also been a source of ideas and a good critical filter.

Many individuals have dedicated thousands of hours in contributing in building the world of Archipelago. Notable are Rob Chin (Xero), David Czeck (Magnifico), Jason Chayes (Ansalon), Chuck Blomberg (Saladin), and Bryan Wride (Ratatosk).

Many of the features and ideas in Archipelago were inspired directly or indirectly by other muds, notably: Perilous Realms, Adversary and Stormbringer.

Questions and suggestion should be directed to Ponder or Kalliste via MUDmail, or to ajn@essex.ac.uk.

CircleMUD was developed from DikuMUD (Gamma 0.0) by Jeremy "Ras" Elson at Johns Hopkins University's Department of Computer Science. All code unique to CircleMUD is protected under a copyright by the Trustees of the Johns Hopkins University.

Many, many people contributed to CircleMUD in one way or another throughout the course of its development. Although it's impossible for me to remember them all, I'd like to extend special thanks to (in alphabetical order): Danny Baker, Bill Bogstad, Harold Ervin, Fenris, Jeff Fink, Furey, Gnort, Sharon Goza, Bryan Jolson, Steven Lacher, Jay Levino, Fred Merkel, Catherine Stanton, Naved Surve, and Tim Stearns. Circle would be different in some way without the contributions of any of these people.

CircleMUD is available for anonymous FTP at ftp.cs.jhu.edu in pub/CircleMUD. Its author can be contacted at jelson@cs.jhu.edu.
